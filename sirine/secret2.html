Experiment Log

Found in a burned down building (possible Red Mantis Hideout)

— Project: Umbra Protocol —

Experiment Log #042

Lead Researcher: Initiate-Prime Vareth Tonn
Field Assistant: Initiate-Cadet Hessa Rul
Location: Subterranean Facility Theta-9, Chamber IV
Subject: Black Ichor Sample 17-C (extracted from corrupted ridgewolf carcass, recovered north of Brann Marsh)
Objective: Controlled exposure of ichor to baseline biological tissue to study corruptive rate, neural override potential, and viability for martial application.

10:02 – Sample 17-C removed from cold storage. Viscosity unchanged. Assistant Rul reports faint resonant vibration in containment vial. Noted. May require runic insulation to limit further ambient bleed.

10:07 – Baseline tissue sample (porcine muscle, preserved) placed in infusion matrix. 1.5 ml of ichor applied via calibrated drip.

10:10 – Immediate response observed. Tissue begins to animate despite lack of neural structure. Uncontrolled convulsions result in partial destruction of matrix. Black growths begin to spread from point of contact—non-organic, likely crystallized necroplasm.

10:12 – Rul notes auditory distortion in chamber: low-frequency murmuring. Confirmed. No discernible language structure. Possibly empathic residue or latent psychic discharge. Standard counter-wards activated.

10:15 – Animation ceases. Tissue mass enters rapid decay phase. Residual ichor remains active, attempting to bond with floor tile grout. Sample removed and incinerated. Floor segment flagged for replacement.

Conclusion: Sample 17-C retains latent autonomous properties even when separated from host. While full control was not achieved, short-term animation and aggression amplification are promising. Further experiments recommended with living subjects and refined binding glyphs. Cadet Rul suggests exploring mirrored runes from the old Sable Lexicon to suppress decay onset—approved for next trial.

Note: Bodily contact with ichor remains prohibited. Acolyte Senn’s incident from Log #039 is not to be repeated.

-END LOG

Experiment Log #088

Lead Researcher: Initiate-Prime Vareth Tonn
Field Assistant: Initiate-Cadet Hessa Rul
Location: Subterranean Facility Theta-9, Chamber VII-B
Subject: Human volunteer (designate: Subject H-03, former conscript, low cognitive resistance per pre-screening)
Objective: Evaluate physiological and cognitive impact of direct ichor injection. Determine viability of corruption retention without full mental dissolution. Combat capability evaluation pending.

09:40 – Subject H-03 restrained and sedated. 5 ml of refined ichor (Batch 21-R, siphoned from stabilized ridgewolf husk) injected intramuscularly in left deltoid. No immediate reaction beyond mild tremors.

09:46 – Sedative wearing off faster than anticipated. Subject vocalizes in fragmented phrases: “pulling,” “through the mirror,” and “the second mouth.” Monitor notes cerebral temperature rising by 3.8°C. Internal warding reinforced.

09:52 – Subject attempts to bite through restraints. Strength exceeds baseline; left wrist dislocated in process. Ichor visibly spreading beneath skin—black vascular patterns resembling branching root systems.

10:01 – Subject enters catatonic state. Pupils dilated to full black. Heart rate irregular but sustained. Assistant Rul notes drop in ambient temperature; condensation forming along outer chamber walls. Not typical.

10:07 – Subject suddenly rises unaided, despite full neuromuscular collapse noted in previous scans. Speech resumes: unfamiliar tongue, pattern consistent with pre-Lexicon glyphwork. Auto-recorders captured full audio. To be analyzed.

10:09 – Subject strikes reinforced glass with bare hand. Fractures appear in wall, along with ichor bloom radiating from impact site. Containment glyphs fail in sequence—Glyphs 3, 6, and 11 lost coherence. Rul activates fail-safe seal. Subject immobilized via arc-blast. Body combusts; black ash remains.

Postmortem Notes: Neural override occurred faster than predicted. Subject retained human form for 11.5 minutes post-injection before total collapse of identity. Ichor exhibits both parasitic and preservative traits—attempting to maintain vessel while eroding autonomy. Result: partial success.

Conclusion: Cognitive retention insufficient. Martial application remains promising in short bursts, but current iterations yield unstable hosts. Recommend continuation with criminal stock, preferably those already exhibiting violent tendencies. Emotional suppression may slow identity erosion. Rul has drafted revised injection matrix with sympathetic glyphs to tether ego. Approved.

-END LOG
