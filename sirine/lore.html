Lore

The world's backstory

Sirine is a world apart—cradled in silence, sealed from the roiling tides of the greater multiverse. Long ago, a force known only as the Grand Aegis encircled it, an arcane barrier woven from forgotten pacts and towering magic. None remember who forged it, nor why, only that its presence has long kept Sirine isolated from planar crossings, celestial traffic, and divine intervention. The stars speak no prophecies here. The gods, if they watch at all, watch from afar.

Life in Sirine adapted. Its peoples grew clever in absence, turning to ancestral memory, ancient rites, and mortal will. Power came not from divine gifts, but from the world itself—from the shadows of the deepwoods, the bones of the mountains, the shifting breath of the sea. For centuries, the Grand Aegis held firm, and Sirine thrived in its quiet defiance.

But now, something has changed.

Years ago, a cataclysm that shook stone and spell alike ocurred at an ancient ruin... The air tastes different. Dreams run longer. Scholars of the Aetheric Colleges speak in hushed tones of hairline fractures in the Grand Aegis—thin places where prayers slip through and strangers slip in. The faithful report flickers of true miracles, brief and burning. Others whisper of horrors that were never meant to set foot in Sirine.

Whatever kept the world sealed is weakening. And not everyone mourns its breaking.
