Races

of the people in Sirine

The world of Sirine is a land shaped by ancient magic, diverse landscapes, and the unique peoples who call it home. From sun-scorched deserts to vast ocean depths and towering forests, its races reflect the power and mystery of their homelands. Each carries its own culture, abilities, and history, woven into the ever-evolving tapestry of Sirine. What follows is a closer look at the races that inhabit this realm.

  • Dwarves:

    • A short, stocky people who are often stubborn, fierce, and devoted. They usualy stick together but do not mind visitors. They are comfortable with the underground, so mining is a common profession.

  • Elves:

    • A tall, long-lived people with a strong tradition of art and magic. Though Half-Elves are much more common, rumors stir that a few Pure Elf individuals have been seen travelling.

  • Humans

    • Diverse and adaptable people with wide potential and deep ambitions.

  • Sarangay

    • Powerful carabao-headed warriors who travel the plains. Known for the magical gems in their foreheads that are said to contain their souls.

  • Leshy

    • Occur when souls are placed in small plant bodies. They typically seek adventure and wonder.

  • Anadi

    • Reclusive, sapient spiders who hail from the jungles. Though they act in many ways like natural-born shapeshifters, their twin forms actually stem from carefully developed magic.

  • Fetchlings

    • Once human and now something apart, fetchlings display the Shadow Plane's ancient influence through monochrome complexions, glowing eyes, and the casting of supernatural shadows.
