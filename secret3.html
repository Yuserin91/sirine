Ancient Journal Entry

Found in Hollowshade Glade; Translated from Ancient Elvish

Journal of Tathren Willowmere

Day 12 of the Bleeding Moon, Year 412 of the Elder Cycle

It has been three moons since the Stillness fell upon Hollowshade Glade. I write this now by firelight, alone beneath a canopy that feels emptier than it ever has in my sixty winters. The forest grieves, and so do I.

The others—my kin, my circle, my heart—they are still. Frozen as though caught mid-breath, eyes open, hands outstretched, in the moment the god died. I saw it happen. I was away from the Glade, gathering ironroot sap in the northern reaches, when the wind died mid-whistle and the birds fell silent. Even the insects ceased their song. The stillness was complete. And then I felt it: a tearing, like bark being peeled from my soul. Curchanus... gone.

We had always believed He was eternal—Lord of the Wild Road, Master of Beasts, Endurer of Time. But something reached beyond the veil and undid Him. I do not know what. I only know that those closest to His sacred grove were taken by that final breath. Frozen where they stood. Not dead, no—there is still a hum in their bones, a flicker of His essence, like coals beneath ash. But unmoving. Unaging.

I tried everything. Chants, blood rites, invocations in the old tongue. I even called to other gods, those we scorned in life. Silence answered. No wind, no song. Just silence.

The Glade is a tomb now. I left offerings, recorded each name I could remember on birchbark, and placed it in the stone circle. I return each season, hoping—though hope is a stubborn weed in barren soil.

Something powerful intervened that day. And if their stillness was meant as protection, or punishment, I cannot say. But I believe it was not the end. The wild does not lie still forever.

If this journal is found, know this: Curchanus walked once, and His children did not die with Him. They wait. And when they wake… may the world be ready.

—T.W.
